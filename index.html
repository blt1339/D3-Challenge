Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

The above chart contrasts demographic factors with health risks for 50 states and the District of Columbia.

This D3 enabled scatterplot allows you click on multiple variables to explore their relationships. Click through each option to see the result!

Observations

  • Obese percent has a positive correlation with poverty percent, no correlation with average age and a negative correlation with average household income.
  • Smokes percent has a positive correlation with poverty, no correlation with average age and a negative correlation with average household income
  • Lacks healthcare percent has a positive correlation with poverty, no correlation with average age and a negative correlation with average household income

As expected, the states or districts with higher poverty rates and therefore lower average income have a higher percent of the risk facters (obesity, smoking, and lack of healthcare). This makes sense since people with more resources can afford to get help to reduce weight, to quit smoking and to buy insurance.
